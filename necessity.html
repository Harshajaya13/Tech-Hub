Trap #0: The Sheep Mentality

‚ñº

Never chase a course just because the crowd is buying it.

Your brain is unique. Your learning style is unique. Just because a roadmap worked for thousands doesn't mean it fits you. If you try to copy someone else's journey, you lose your own destination.

Don't compare your Chapter 1 to someone else's Chapter 20.

Looking at a Senior Dev and feeling small is self-sabotage. They have years of invisible failures you haven't seen. The only valid comparison is You vs. You Yesterday. If you are 1% better than your past self, you are winning.

Trap #1: The "Senior" Echo Chamber

‚ñº

The biggest mistake? Blindly asking a senior "Which language is best?" and following it without asking "Why?"

They suggest what worked for them, or what was trending last year. You pick it up, you hate it, and you quit halfway through. Their path is not your blueprint.

Trap #2: The Infinite Ocean

‚ñº

Imagine you are on a boat crossing an ocean. Halfway there, you notice the seats aren't comfortable.

Would you jump into the sea just to swim to another boat?

No. You would drown. Yet, students do this every day. They learn Python for 2 weeks, get bored, jump to Java, get stuck, jump to Web Dev. You never reach the shore if you keep changing boats. Pick one vessel and sail it to the end.

Trap #3: The Regret Loop

‚ñº

Lack of clarity leads to "trying everything." You spend your first year dabbling. By the time you realize what you actually enjoy, your time is up.

The Danger: Graduating with a "basic knowledge" of 5 things, but mastery of zero. The market doesn't pay for "basics."

Trap #4: The Tutorial Coma

‚ñº

You watch a 10-hour course. You nod your head. You feel smart. Then you open a blank code editor, and your mind goes blank.

Watching is not Doing.

You don't learn by watching a master. You learn by struggling in the mud. If you aren't frustrated, you aren't learning. Close the video. Break things.

Trap #5: The AI Excuse

‚ñº

"Why learn to code if AI can do it for me?"

AI replaces "Coders." It creates "Architects."

If you just learn syntax, AI will replace you in 2 years. But if you learn logic, system design, and problem-solving, AI becomes your superpower, not your replacement. Don't use the future as an excuse to be lazy in the present.

The Golden Rule: Builders Win

‚ñº

Watching a tutorial on swimming won't keep you from drowning. Watching a tutorial on Python won't make you a developer.

Stop Consuming. Start Creating.

The real world doesn't care about your certificates or how many videos you watched. It cares about what you have built. If your GitHub is empty, your knowledge is invisible.
